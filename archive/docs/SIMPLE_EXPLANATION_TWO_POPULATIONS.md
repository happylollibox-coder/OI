# Simple Explanation: Two Populations Analysis

## What We're Looking For

We're finding two different types of ASIN + search term combinations:

### Population 1: Mostly Organic Performers
**What**: ASIN + search terms that sell well organically with very few paid clicks

**Criteria**:
- At least 20 orders in the last 6 months
- Very few paid clicks (mostly organic sales)
- Show monthly: how many clicks per order

**Why**: These are terms that work well organically - you might not need to spend much on ads!

---

### Population 2: Paid Clicks Drive Organic Orders
**What**: ASIN + search terms where spending on ads helps organic sales grow

**Criteria**:
- At least 20 orders in the last 6 months
- At least 100 paid clicks in the last 6 months
- When paid clicks go up, organic orders also go up
- Show monthly: how many clicks per order

**Why**: These are terms where your ad investment is helping organic sales - keep investing!

---

## What You'll See in the Results

### For Both Populations:

**Monthly Breakdown** (last 6 months):
- Each month shown separately
- **Clicks per order** for that month (smaller is better!)
  - Example: 3 clicks per order = excellent ✅
  - Example: 10 clicks per order = needs improvement 📊

**6-Month Summary**:
- Total orders in 6 months
- Total clicks in 6 months
- Overall clicks per order (average)

---

## How to Read the Results

### Population 1: Mostly Organic

**Look for**:
- ✅ Very low paid clicks (≤50 total)
- ✅ High organic orders (80%+ of total orders)
- ✅ Low clicks per order (≤5 is good)
- ✅ Consistent month-to-month

**Example**:
- ASIN: B123456789
- Search Term: "wireless headphones"
- Total Orders: 25
- Paid Clicks: 10 (very low!)
- Organic Orders: 24 (96% organic!)
- Clicks per Order: 4.2 ✅ (excellent!)

**What this means**: This term sells great organically - you don't need to spend much on ads!

---

### Population 2: Paid Drives Organic

**Look for**:
- ✅ High paid clicks (≥100 total)
- ✅ Positive correlation (when paid goes up, organic goes up)
- ✅ Low clicks per order (≤5 is good)
- ✅ Correlation ≥ 0.3 (moderate to strong relationship)

**Example**:
- ASIN: B987654321
- Search Term: "bluetooth speaker"
- Total Orders: 35
- Paid Clicks: 150
- Correlation: 0.65 🔥 (strong!)
- Clicks per Order: 5.8 ✅ (good!)

**What this means**: When you spend more on ads for this term, organic sales also increase - keep investing!

---

## Key Metric: Clicks Per Order

**What it means**: How many clicks (paid + organic) it takes to get 1 order

**Smaller is Better**:
- **≤3 clicks per order**: 🔥 Excellent - Very efficient!
- **≤5 clicks per order**: ✅ Good - Efficient
- **≤10 clicks per order**: 💡 Moderate - Could be better
- **>10 clicks per order**: 📊 High - Needs optimization

**Example**:
- Month 1: 20 clicks, 5 orders = 4 clicks per order ✅
- Month 2: 30 clicks, 4 orders = 7.5 clicks per order 💡
- Month 3: 15 clicks, 6 orders = 2.5 clicks per order 🔥

---

## How to Use These Results

### For Population 1 (Mostly Organic):
1. **Identify** terms with low clicks per order
2. **Focus** on organic optimization (SEO, content)
3. **Reduce** paid spend (not needed much)
4. **Monitor** to maintain organic performance

### For Population 2 (Paid Drives Organic):
1. **Identify** terms with strong correlation
2. **Increase** paid investment (it helps organic!)
3. **Monitor** clicks per order (keep it low)
4. **Scale** campaigns that work
