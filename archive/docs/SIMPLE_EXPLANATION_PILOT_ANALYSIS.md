# Simple Explanation: Finding Pilot Candidates

## What We're Looking For

Imagine you're testing which search terms are worth investing more money in. We want to find terms where:

**5 paid clicks = 1 order** (either from the paid ad or from organic search)

## Why This Matters

If a search term consistently gives you 1 order for every 5 clicks you pay for, you can:
- Predict how many orders you'll get
- Set your budget confidently
- Scale up campaigns knowing the results

## The Three Things We Check

### 1. Does It Meet the 5:1 Ratio? (Most Important)

**What we check**: Over the last 6 months, does this search term give you at least 1 order for every 5 paid clicks?

**Example**:
- Search term: "wireless headphones"
- Last 6 months: 100 paid clicks, 22 orders
- Calculation: 22 orders ÷ 100 clicks = 0.22
- Result: ✅ YES! (0.22 is better than 0.20, which is 1 order per 5 clicks)

**What we want**: 0.20 or higher (the higher, the better)

---

### 2. Has It Been Consistent? (Reliability Check)

**What we check**: Out of the last 6 months, how many months did this search term meet the 5:1 target?

**Example**:
- January: ✅ Met target (5 clicks = 1 order)
- February: ✅ Met target
- March: ❌ Didn't meet (needed 7 clicks for 1 order)
- April: ✅ Met target
- May: ✅ Met target
- June: ✅ Met target
- Result: 5 out of 6 months = 83% consistency

**What we want**: 60% or higher (the higher, the better)
- 80%+ = Very reliable ✅
- 60-80% = Reliable ✅
- 40-60% = Sometimes works 💡
- Below 40% = Unreliable ❌

---

### 3. Was It Better This Time Last Year? (Seasonality Check)

**What we check**: Did this search term perform better in the same month last year?

**Example**:
- This June: 50 clicks, 11 orders = 0.22 ratio
- Last June: 45 clicks, 8 orders = 0.18 ratio
- Result: ✅ YES! Better this year (0.22 vs 0.18)

**Why this matters**: 
- If it was better last year, it might be a seasonal product (like Christmas items)
- If it's better now, it's improving
- If it's worse now, it might be declining

**What we want**: Better than last year, or at least similar

---

## How We Score Each Search Term

We give each search term a score from 0 to 100:

**Score Breakdown**:
- **Ratio Quality** (0-40 points): How good is the 5:1 ratio?
  - Perfect (0.3+) = 40 points
  - Meets target (0.2) = 30 points ✅
  - Close (0.15) = 20 points
  
- **Consistency** (0-30 points): How reliable is it?
  - Very consistent (80%+) = 30 points
  - Consistent (60%+) = 25 points ✅
  - Moderate (40%+) = 20 points
  
- **Seasonality** (0-20 points): How does it compare to last year?
  - Much better (20%+) = 20 points
  - Better = 10 points ✅
  - Same or worse = 5 points
  
- **Volume** (0-10 points): How much data do we have?
  - Lots of clicks (100+) = 10 points
  - Good amount (50+) = 8 points
  - Enough (25+) = 6 points ✅

**Total Score**:
- **80-100**: 🔥 EXCELLENT - Use these first!
- **60-79**: ✅ STRONG - Great candidates
- **40-59**: 💡 GOOD - Consider these
- **Below 40**: 📊 MONITOR - Not ready yet

---

## What You'll See in the Results

For each search term, you'll see:

1. **Pilot Candidate Score** (0-100): The overall score
2. **6-Month Ratio**: How many orders per click (want 0.20+)
3. **Consistency**: How many months met target (want 60%+)
4. **Last Year Comparison**: Better, same, or worse
5. **Recommendation**: EXCELLENT/STRONG/GOOD/MONITOR

---

## How to Use the Results

### Step 1: Look for High Scores
Focus on search terms with **score ≥ 70**

### Step 2: Check the Ratio
Make sure **6-month ratio ≥ 0.20** (meets 5:1 target)

### Step 3: Check Consistency
Make sure **consistency ≥ 60%** (reliable)

### Step 4: Check Seasonality
Prefer terms that are **better than last year**

### Step 5: Create Pilot Campaigns
Use the top 20-50 search terms for your pilot

## Real Example

**Search Term**: "bluetooth speaker"

**Results**:
- 6-Month Ratio: 0.24 (1 order per 4.2 clicks) ✅
- Consistency: 5 out of 6 months (83%) ✅
- Last Year: 18% better this year ✅
- Score: 82 points 🔥
- Recommendation: EXCELLENT PILOT CANDIDATE

**What this means**: 
This search term is excellent because:
1. It gives you MORE than 1 order per 5 clicks (actually 1 per 4.2)
2. It's been consistent 83% of the time
3. It's performing better than last year
4. High score means it's a top priority

**Action**: Create a pilot campaign with this term and expect good results!

## What Makes a Good Pilot Candidate?

✅ **Must Have**:
- Ratio ≥ 0.20 (meets 5:1 target)
- Consistency ≥ 60% (reliable)
- Score ≥ 60 (strong candidate)

🔥 **Nice to Have**:
- Ratio ≥ 0.25 (better than 5:1)
- Consistency ≥ 80% (very reliable)
- Better than last year (improving)
- Score ≥ 70 (excellent candidate)

## Summary

We're finding search terms that:
1. Give you 1 order for every 5 paid clicks (or better)
2. Do this consistently month after month
3. Are performing well compared to last year

These are the terms you should test in a pilot campaign because they're proven to work!
