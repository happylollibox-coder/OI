# Recommended Action Plan — Easiest Path to Higher Chances of Winning

**Goal:** Maximize the chance Amazon takes action on the trade dress infringement (ASIN B0G1G5343H) with the **least effort** that gives **meaningfully higher** odds.

## Option A: Do This First (Easiest — Do Today)

**No new filings. Just make your current report as strong as possible.**

1. **Report via Brand Registry** using your **word mark** (HAPPY LOLLI).
2. **Include ASIN B0G1G5343H** — check “Include in report” even if you see the yellow warning; choose “Product images” (or “Product packaging”) in the location dropdown.
3. **Content types:** Check **Product images** (and **Product description** if the listing text also copies your structure).
4. **“What best describes your issue?”** → Choose **“A product detail page is unlawfully using my trademark (e.g. in product title, product images, product description).”**
5. **Paste the full 149-word trade dress description** in the violation/additional information field (from the Appendix of HAPPY_LOLLI_Trade_Dress_Infringement_Report.md).
6. **Attach evidence** if the form allows:
   - One side-by-side image: your main image (B0F4KCCSWN) next to the reported listing’s main image (B0G1G5343H).
   - Optional: one-page comparison (e.g. from HAPPY_LOLLI_Trade_Dress_Evidence_Comparison.md) with color codes or short bullet points.

**Why:** Free, quick, and gives a human reviewer the best chance to understand this is trade dress (overall look), not just “they didn’t use our name.” Evidence makes the similarity obvious.

**Chances:** Still limited by the word-mark framing, but **better than a bare report** with no description or evidence.

## Option B: The One Move That Makes Your Chances Much Higher (Recommended)

**Get a design mark registration for your label or main image, then report with it.**

- **What to register:** Your **label design** or **main product image** (the distinctive graphic/layout consumers see — e.g. the tub + sticks + “60 Truths & Dares” layout as a single design). File as a **design mark** (special form), not a word mark.
- **Why design mark instead of full trade dress:**  
  - One clear drawing, one specimen (e.g. packaging photo or Amazon main image).  
  - Fewer distinctiveness/functionality arguments than full “trade dress” packaging.  
  - Once registered, you select **this** registration in Brand Registry when reporting; the ASIN is much less likely to be excluded and reviewers can match “design” to “they copied our design.”
- **Steps:** See [How_to_Register_Design_or_Trade_Dress_Trademark.md](How_to_Register_Design_or_Trade_Dress_Trademark.md) — use the “Design mark” path (drawing of the label/main image, description, specimen, one class of goods). File via USPTO Trademark Center or TEAS.
- **Timeline:** Several months for registration. After it registers, add it to Brand Registry and submit a **new** report against B0G1G5343H (or future lookalikes) using this design registration.

**Why:** This is the **easiest registration path** that aligns your IP right with your claim (“they copied our design”) and **significantly increases** the chance Amazon takes action.

## Option C: Full Packaging Trade Dress (Strongest, More Work)

- Register the **overall packaging trade dress** (container + sticks + colors + layout as one trade dress).
- Strongest long-term and best match to “look and feel,” but more description, possible office actions on distinctiveness/functionality, and more back-and-forth. Do this if you want maximum protection and are willing to invest more time or use an attorney.
