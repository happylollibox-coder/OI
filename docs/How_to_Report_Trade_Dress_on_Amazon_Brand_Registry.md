# How to Report Trade Dress Infringement on Amazon Brand Registry

Use this guide with the **Report a violation** tool at [brandregistry.amazon.com](https://brandregistry.amazon.com) → **Protect** → **Report a violation**.  
**Reported ASIN:** B0G1G5343H | **Your product:** B0F4KCCSWN (HAPPY LOLLI Truth or Dare)

---

## Step 1: Choose infringement type

In the **“Report 1 selected”** (or similar) dropdown:

- Select **“Trademark infringement”**.

Trade dress is a type of trademark under U.S. law, so this is the correct category (not Copyright or Patent).

---

## Step 2: Brand and trademark

- **Brand name:** Select **HAPPY LOLLI**.
- **Which trademark is being infringed?**  
  If your account has a **design / figurative or trade dress** registration, select that.  
  If you only have a **word mark** (e.g. “6953098 - word mar”), you can still proceed; use the description in Step 5 to clarify that the report is for **trade dress** (overall visual appearance), not use of the word “HAPPY LOLLI.”

### Word mark vs. design/trade dress registration — chances of success

| Basis for report | Chances of Amazon taking action | Why |
|------------------|----------------------------------|-----|
| **Word mark only** | **Lower** | Amazon’s system looks for use of the *text* of your mark (e.g. “HAPPY LOLLI”). The other listing uses “TIFFYONEK,” so the ASIN is often **auto-excluded** (“do not contain the reported trademark”). Even if you include it and explain trade dress, reviewers may treat the case as “no word mark on the page” and deny. Your claim is legally valid (trade dress under trademark law) but the **tool and review process are not built for trade dress when only a word mark is registered**. |
| **Design / figurative or trade dress registration** | **Higher** | The registration protects *visual appearance* (logo, packaging design, or product dress). The infringement you report (copying the look, images, layout) **matches** the right you assert. The ASIN is less likely to be excluded, and reviewers can compare the registered design to the reported listing. This gives a **much better chance** of removal or corrective action. |

**Bottom line:** If you have or can obtain a **design or trade dress** registration for the product/packaging look (or a distinctive logo used on it), use that when reporting. If you only have a word mark, you can still submit and explain trade dress in the description, but expect a lower success rate; consider pursuing a design or trade dress registration for stronger enforcement. See **[How to Register Design or Trade Dress Trademark](How_to_Register_Design_or_Trade_Dress_Trademark.md)** for the application process.

## Step 3: What content is infringing?

In the checklist of listing elements:

- **Check “Product images”** — the main way trade dress is copied (overall look, layout, colors, packaging).
- **Check “Product description”** if the listing text copies your structure, “60 Truths & Dares” presentation, or niche wording.
- Leave unchecked: Title, Brand name (the other listing uses “TIFFYONEK”), etc., unless you have a separate claim.

---

## Step 4: Add the ASIN and keep it in the report

- Enter or confirm ASIN **B0G1G5343H** (reported product).
- If you see a **yellow warning** that the ASIN “do not contain the reported trademark” and it is excluded:
  - That message appears because the other listing does **not** use the text “HAPPY LOLLI” (it uses “TIFFYONEK”).
  - For **trade dress**, the infringement is the **overall visual similarity** (images, packaging look), not the word mark.
  - **Include the ASIN in the report:** check **“Include in report”** for B0G1G5343H.
  - In **“Location of reported trademark”** (or “Where is the trademark used?”), choose the option that best fits, e.g. **“Product images”** or **“Product packaging”** / **“Product”**, and use the **Additional information / Violation description** field (Step 5) to state clearly that this is a **trade dress** claim.

---

## Step 5: “What best describes your issue?”

Choose the option that best matches your claim:

- **“A product detail page is unlawfully using my trademark (e.g. in product title, product images, product description).”**  
  Use this if you are focusing on the **listing** (main image, layout, colors, “60 Truths & Dares” presentation) as mimicking your trade dress.

- **“A product or its packaging has my trademark on it.”**  
  Use this if you are focusing on the **physical product/packaging** (container, sticks, colors) as mimicking your trade dress.

You can select the one that fits best; many trade dress reports use the **product detail page** option when the main evidence is the listing images.

---

## Step 6: Violation description (required / additional information)

In the text field for describing the violation, paste the following (or adapt from your main report). This makes clear the report is **trade dress**, not word-mark, and helps avoid the ASIN being excluded:

**Trade dress infringement — HAPPY LOLLI**

The reported listing (ASIN B0G1G5343H) infringes HAPPY LOLLI’s trade dress by mimicking the overall visual identity of our product and listing (ASIN B0F4KCCSWN). Our protectable trade dress consists of the combined, nonfunctional presentation of: champagne-style bottle packaging (pastel pink body, mint green cap and base, twist-open bottom), label design (“TRUTH OR DARE,” tagline “The Game That Starts with a Twist and Ends with OMG!,” speech bubbles), mesh bow and silver ribbons, color palette (pink and mint on bottle and on 30 Truth + 30 Dare sticks), flat sticks with heart icons, and visual positioning toward the same consumer niche (girls’ sleepover/party). The reported listing replicates or closely imitates this combination, creating a similar overall “look and feel.” In the Amazon context—where consumers rely heavily on thumbnail images and category browsing—this similarity is likely to cause confusion as to source or affiliation. We request review under Amazon Brand Registry and appropriate action. We do not assert claims based on the words “truth or dare” or on functional product features alone.

*(~150 words — fits typical limits.)*

## Step 7: Submit

- Review that ASIN **B0G1G5343H** is **included** in the report (not excluded).
- Attach any evidence (e.g. side-by-side images, color comparison) if the form allows.
- Click **Submit**.

## If the form still excludes the ASIN

- Use **“Include in report”** and any **“Location”** dropdown to tie the report to **Product images** or **Product/packaging**.
- In every available text field, state clearly: **“This report is for trade dress infringement (overall visual appearance and product/packaging dress), not for use of the word mark HAPPY LOLLI. The infringing listing copies the distinctive look and feel of our product and listing images.”**
- If Brand Registry has a separate path for “Design patent or trade dress,” use that instead; otherwise **Trademark infringement** plus the description above is the correct path.

For full legal wording and evidence structure, use **HAPPY_LOLLI_Trade_Dress_Infringement_Report.md** and **HAPPY_LOLLI_Trade_Dress_Evidence_Comparison.md**.

## Exact steps when you have only a WORD MARK (reporting trade dress)

Use this when you are on the **Report a Violation** page, the ASIN is listed (e.g. B0G1G5343H), and you have **“Location of reported trademark”** and **“Please provide more information”** on screen.

### 1. ASIN summary

- **Include in report:** Leave **checked** for the ASIN you are reporting (e.g. B0G1G5343H).

### 2. “Location of reported trademark (required)” dropdown

- **Do not** leave “Product description” if the main infringement is visual.
- **Select:** **“Product images”** (or **“Product packaging”** / **“Product”** if those options appear).  
  That tells Amazon the infringement is the **look** of the product/listing, not the text of your word mark.
- If only “Product description” is available, select it but then **state clearly in the text box below** that the report is for **trade dress** (overall visual imitation), and that the similar **images** and **packaging appearance** are what cause confusion.

### 3. “What best describes the products in your report?”

- **Check:** **“Your brand’s authentic product is being imitated.”**  
  This fits trade dress (they are imitating the overall look of your product/packaging).
- **Uncheck** “Description” if it is only about text and your main claim is visual (images/packaging).

### 4. “Please provide more information to help us understand your issue”

**Paste the text below** into this box (you can shorten if there is a character limit). This explains that you are reporting **trade dress**, not use of the word “HAPPY LOLLI”:

```
We are reporting trade dress infringement, not use of our word mark "HAPPY LOLLI." The reported listing (ASIN B0G1G5343H) does not use our brand name; it imitates the overall visual appearance of our product and listing (ASIN B0F4KCCSWN).

Our trade dress is the distinctive combination of: (1) champagne-style bottle packaging (tall, slender bottle with neck and shoulders, pastel pink body, mint green cap and base, twist-open bottom for sticks); (2) label design with "TRUTH OR DARE" and tagline "The Game That Starts with a Twist and Ends with OMG!"; (3) mesh bow with silver stars at the neck; (4) color palette (pastel pink and mint green on bottle and on game sticks—pink for Truth, mint for Dare); (5) flat sticks with rounded ends, heart icons, and same count (30 Truth + 30 Dare); (6) same product format and niche (girls' sleepover/party games). The reported listing copies this overall look and feel—same bottle/container styling, same pink-and-mint colors, same stick presentation and layout—so customers are likely to be confused about source when viewing thumbnails and search results on Amazon.

We selected "Product images" (or Product packaging) as the location because the infringement is the visual similarity of the listing images and packaging, not the product title or brand name. We request review and action under your IP policy.
```

**If the box has a 3000-character limit,** the text above is under 3000 characters; you can add a sentence such as: “We can provide side-by-side image comparison or additional evidence if needed.”

### 5. Submit

- Click **Submit**. Keep a screenshot of the confirmation and the report details for your records.
