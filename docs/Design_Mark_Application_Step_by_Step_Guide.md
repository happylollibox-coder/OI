# Design Mark Application — Step-by-Step Guide (Copy/Paste Ready)

**Purpose:** File a U.S. design mark application for HAPPY LOLLI’s product/label design so you can report infringement on Amazon using this registration.  
**Where you file:** [Trademark Center](https://trademarkcenter.uspto.gov/) (as of Jan 2025, this is where new applications are filed.)

## A2. Drawing (the “mark”) — What to prepare

- **What it is:** A single image that shows **only** the design you want to register (e.g. your label, or your product/packaging as a flat graphic).
- **Format:** JPG (RGB, not CMYK).
- **Size:** Under 5 MB. Recommended: 250–944 pixels on the longest side, 300–350 DPI if possible. Lines clean and sharp.
- **Content:** Design on **white or light background**. Minimal empty space around the design. If you claim **color**, the drawing must be in color.
- **Where to get it:** Export from your design file, or take a clear photo of the label/packaging on a white background and crop to the design only. Save as `HAPPY_LOLLI_design_mark.jpg` (or similar).

**If you’re not sure:** Use your **Amazon main image** (product + packaging) cropped to show the distinctive layout (container + sticks + “60 Truths & Dares” area). It must look like a single, consistent “mark,” not a full product photo with lots of background.

## A3. Specimen (proof of use) — What to prepare

- **What it is:** Proof that you **already use** this design in commerce (e.g. on the product, packaging, or a listing).
- **Acceptable:** (1) Photo of your product/packaging with the design visible, or (2) Screenshot of your Amazon listing showing the product (include URL and date in the application if the form asks).
- **Rule:** The design in the specimen must match the design in the drawing (same overall look).
- **Format:** JPG or PNG, under 5 MB.

**Suggested:** Screenshot of [your HAPPY LOLLI listing](https://www.amazon.com/HAPPY-LOLLI-Truth-Dare-Game/dp/B0F4KCCSWN) showing the main image, or a photo of the physical packaging. Save as `HAPPY_LOLLI_specimen.jpg`.

## A4. Copy/paste text you will need

Copy these into a separate document so you can paste them into the form. Replace any `[BRACKETED]` parts with your information.

### Mark description (required for design/special form)

Paste this in the field that asks for a **description of the mark** (e.g. “The mark consists of:” or “Describe the mark”):

```
The mark consists of the design of product packaging comprising a cylindrical container with a clear lid displaying multiple colored sticks, and a label area featuring the wording "60 Truths & Dares" in stylized text, with a distinctive layout, color scheme, and graphic elements that together create the overall commercial impression of the packaging. The colors [INSERT IF YOU CLAIM COLOR, e.g. "pink, yellow, blue, and white"] are claimed as a feature of the mark.
```

**If you do NOT claim color** (black-and-white drawing), use this instead:

```
The mark consists of the design of product packaging comprising a cylindrical container with a clear lid displaying multiple colored sticks, and a label area featuring the wording "60 Truths & Dares" in stylized text, with a distinctive layout and graphic elements that together create the overall commercial impression of the packaging.
```

**If your actual design is different** (e.g. only a logo, no container): Replace the text above with a short description of what is in your drawing (all text and design elements, and where colors are if you claim color).

### Color claim (only if your drawing is in color)

If the drawing is in **color** and you want to claim color as part of the mark, find the field that asks **“Is color claimed as a feature of the mark?”** or similar, and:

- Select **Yes**.
- In the field that asks **which colors**, type exactly (adjust to match your design):

```
Pink, yellow, blue, white, and [any other colors in your drawing].
```

---

### Goods (identification of goods) — Class 28

In the **Goods and Services** section, you must choose **Class 28** and use an identification from the USPTO’s ID Manual. **Search** in the application for “games” or “party” and pick one that matches, **or** type one of these if the form allows free text (many forms require picking from the manual):

**Option 1 (recommended):**  
`Board games`

**Option 2:**  
`Party games`

**Option 3:**  
`Tabletop games`

If the form only allows selections from the ID Manual: search for **“board games”** or **“games”** in Class 28 and select the listing that best fits (e.g. “Board games”).

---

### Verification statement (you will sign)

The form will ask you to confirm something like: “The signatory is the applicant, or a person authorized to sign on behalf of the applicant, and the signatory has read and agrees that the application is being submitted under the signatory’s signature.” You will **check a box** and **type your name** (and sometimes title). Have ready:

- **Your full name** (as signatory): `[YOUR NAME]`
- **Your title** (if company): e.g. `Owner`, `CEO`, `Authorized Officer`

## A5. Fee

- **Amount:** Check current fee at [USPTO trademark fees](https://www.uspto.gov/trademarks/fees-payment-information/overview-trademark-fees). One class (Class 28) = one fee (e.g. in the $250–$350 range depending on form type; confirm on USPTO site).
- **Payment:** Credit card or USPTO deposit account. Have the card or account ready.

# PART B: USPTO ACCOUNT AND LOGIN

---

## B1. Create or log in to USPTO.gov account

1. Go to: **https://account.uspto.gov/profile/create-account** (or [uspto.gov](https://www.uspto.gov) → Sign in / Create account).
2. If you already have an account: **Log in** and go to B2.
3. If not: Click **Create account**. Enter email, password, and any required fields. Complete **multifactor authentication** (e.g. phone or app) when prompted.
4. When asked, complete **identity verification** (one-time). Follow the on-screen steps (e.g. upload ID or answer questions). This is required to file.

---

## B2. Open Trademark Center

1. Go to: **https://trademarkcenter.uspto.gov/**
2. Log in with your USPTO.gov account if not already logged in.
3. On the main page, find and click the option to **file a new application** (wording may be “File new application,” “Apply for a trademark,” or “Start new application”). Click it.

**If you are sent to TEAS instead:** Look for “Initial application” or “New application” and choose the form for a **trademark** (not service mark only). Then follow the same logic below: applicant info, mark type, drawing, goods, specimen, fee, sign.

- **Email:** Address for USPTO correspondence.
- **Phone:** Optional but recommended.

## C2. Mark type / drawing type

- The form will ask **what type of mark** or **drawing type**.
- Select: **“Special form”** or **“Stylized and/or design”** or **“Design mark”** (wording varies).  
- **Do not** select “Standard character” or “Word mark only.”

---

## C3. Upload the drawing (image of the mark)

- Find the section that asks for the **trademark drawing** or **image of the mark**.
- Click **Choose File** / **Upload** / **Attach** and select your **drawing JPG** (from A2).
- Confirm the file appears (name or thumbnail). If there is a “Preview,” check that the design looks correct and is not blurred or cropped badly.

---

## C4. Mark description

- Find the field that says **“The mark consists of:”** or **“Description of the mark”** or **“Describe all elements of the mark.”**
- **Paste** the exact description you prepared in **A4** (Mark description). If you did not claim color in A4, use the version **without** the color sentence. If you did claim color, use the version **with** the color sentence and the correct color names.

---

## C5. Color (only if your drawing is in color)

- If there is a question like **“Do you claim color as a feature of the mark?”** and your drawing is in color: select **Yes**.
- In the field for **which colors**, paste the color list you prepared in A4 (e.g. “Pink, yellow, blue, white, and [others]”).
- If your drawing is **black and white**, select **No** (or leave unchecked).

---

## C6. Goods and services (Class 28)

- Find **“Goods and Services”** or **“Classification”** or **“Identification of goods.”**
- Add **one class**: **Class 28** (Games, toys, sporting goods).
- For the **identification**, either:
  - **Search** the ID Manual in the form for “board games” or “games” in Class 28 and select the best match, **or**
  - **Type** (if allowed): `Board games`
- Do **not** add extra classes unless you really have goods in another class. One class = one fee.

---

## C7. Filing basis

- You will be asked for **filing basis**: use in commerce vs. intent to use.
- **If you already sell** the product (e.g. on Amazon): select **“Use in commerce”** or **“Section 1(a)”**.
- **If you have not yet sold** the product with this design: select **“Intent to use”** or **“Section 1(b).”** You will have to submit a specimen later (after notice of allowance).

**For HAPPY LOLLI (already on Amazon):** Choose **Use in commerce (1(a)).**

---

## C8. Specimen (required for “use in commerce”)

- Only for **Section 1(a) / Use in commerce**.
- Find **“Specimen”** or **“Proof of use.”**
- **Upload** your specimen file (from A3), or provide the **URL** of the webpage where the mark is used (e.g. your Amazon listing) and the **date** you accessed it.
- If you use a URL: paste `https://www.amazon.com/HAPPY-LOLLI-Truth-Dare-Game/dp/B0F4KCCSWN` and the date (e.g. March 1, 2026).
- Confirm the specimen shows the **same** design as in your drawing.

---

## C9. Declaration / verification and signature

- Find the **verification** or **declaration** section. You will see a statement that the information is true and that the signatory is authorized to sign.
- **Check the box** to agree.
- **Sign:** Usually by typing your full name, and sometimes selecting “Applicant” or “Authorized signatory.” Some forms use a separate “Sign” button.
- Enter **date** (often auto-filled).

**Signatory name:** `[YOUR FULL NAME]`  
**Title (if company):** `[e.g. Owner, CEO]`

---

## C10. Review and validate

- Use **“Validate”** or **“Check for errors”** if the form has it. Fix any errors (missing fields, file size, etc.).
- Scroll through the whole form and confirm:
  - Applicant name and address correct
  - Drawing uploaded and description matches
  - Class 28 and goods correct
  - Specimen uploaded or URL + date
  - Signature and date

---

## C11. Pay and submit

- Click **“Pay and Submit”** or **“Submit”** (you may be taken to a payment screen first).
- Enter payment details (credit card or deposit account). Confirm the **fee amount** (one class).
- Complete payment. You should see a **confirmation page** and receive a **serial number** (application number). Save or screenshot it.
- Check the **email** associated with your USPTO account for a **filing receipt** (often within a few days). Keep that receipt; it has your serial number and filing date.

# IF SOMETHING IS MISSING

- **Drawing:** Use a clear image of your label or main product image (white background, JPG, under 5 MB). You can refine later with an attorney if you get an office action.
- **Specimen:** Use a screenshot of your Amazon listing (same design as drawing) with URL and date.
- **Applicant:** Use the exact legal name of the owner (you or your company). Correct later via a change of owner form if needed.
- **Goods:** “Board games” in Class 28 is acceptable; you can narrow or broaden later only within limits (amendments may be required).

If the **form labels or flow** differ from this guide (e.g. “Trademark Center” looks different or you are on TEAS), use the **same information** and look for the **closest matching field** (applicant, mark type, drawing, description, goods, basis, specimen, signature, payment). When in doubt, use the [Trademark Assistance Center](https://www.uspto.gov/trademarks/contact-trademarks): 1-800-786-9199 (option 1).
