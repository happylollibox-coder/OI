## I. Executive Summary

HAPPY LOLLI submits this report to request review of the reported product listing (ASIN B0G1G5343H) for trade dress infringement under Amazon Brand Registry. The reported listing presents a combined visual identity that closely mimics HAPPY LOLLI’s distinctive trade dress in product and listing presentation. The overall “look and feel”—including main image composition, packaging structure, color palette, typography and layout, product format (central container with colored sticks), presentation of “60 Truths & Dares,” and positioning toward the same consumer niche—creates a likelihood of consumer confusion in the Amazon marketplace context. This report identifies HAPPY LOLLI’s protectable trade dress, compares it to the reported listing, applies a likelihood-of-confusion analysis, and requests that Amazon take appropriate action under Brand Registry.

## II. Our Distinctive Trade Dress (Overall Visual Identity)

HAPPY LOLLI’s trade dress consists of the **nonfunctional combination** of visual elements that together identify the source of the goods and distinguish them from others in the same category. The following elements, in combination, form our distinctive trade dress:

### A. Protectable Combined Elements

1. **Main image composition**  
   The specific arrangement of product, packaging, and supporting elements in the primary listing image (e.g., angle, framing, relationship of container to sticks and to any text or graphics) that yields a recognizable overall impression.

2. **Packaging structure**  
   The overall shape, configuration, and structure of the packaging: champagne-style bottle (tall, slender, with neck and shoulders, pastel pink body, mint green cap and base), twist-open bottom for dispensing sticks, mesh bow with silver stars at the neck, and silver ribbons—as presented to consumers. The combination of bottle shape, two-tone pink-and-mint coloring, and decorative elements is nonfunctional and distinctive.

3. **Color palette and tonal similarity**  
   The particular combination and use of colors (e.g., on packaging, sticks, and listing graphics) that, together, create a consistent and identifiable visual identity for HAPPY LOLLI in this product category.

4. **Typography style and layout**  
   The choice of typeface(s), sizing, placement, and hierarchy of text (e.g., “60 Truths & Dares,” brand name, and any taglines) as they appear on the product and in the listing imagery.

5. **Product format**  
   The visual presentation of the product as a champagne-style game bottle with 30 pink (Truth) and 30 mint (Dare) flat sticks with rounded ends and heart icons, presented in a way that is distinctive rather than dictated solely by function.

6. **Presentation of “60 Truths & Dares”**  
   The specific manner in which the “60 Truths & Dares” (or equivalent count) concept is communicated—through wording, placement, and graphic treatment—as part of the overall listing and product appearance.

7. **Visual positioning toward the girls’ sleepover niche**  
   The combined visual cues (imagery, colors, wording, and layout) that signal the product as intended for the same audience (e.g., girls’ sleepover / party / tween segment), as part of the overall commercial impression.

### B. Generic or Functional Elements (Not Claimed as Trade Dress)

- The idea of a “truth or dare” or similar party game.  
- The use of a container and separate pieces (sticks, cards, etc.) where that format is functional.  
- The number “60” or similar count where it merely describes quantity.  
- Single colors or basic shapes used in a purely functional or descriptive way.  
- Any element that is standard in the industry and not used by HAPPY LOLLI in a distinctive combination.

## IV. Likelihood of Consumer Confusion

### A. Legal Standard (Trade Dress)

Likelihood of confusion is the touchstone for trade dress infringement. The analysis focuses on whether an appreciable number of ordinarily prudent purchasers are likely to be confused as to the source, sponsorship, or affiliation of the reported product when viewed in the actual marketplace context.

### B. Factors Applied to Amazon Context

1. **Strength of HAPPY LOLLI’s trade dress**  
   Our trade dress is [inherently distinctive and/or has acquired distinctiveness through use and exposure]. The combination of the elements described in Section II is not functional or generic in the aggregate and serves to identify HAPPY LOLLI.

2. **Similarity of the trade dress**  
   As set out in Section III, the reported listing (B0G1G5343H) replicates or closely imitates the combination of main image composition, packaging structure, color palette, typography and layout, product format, “60 Truths & Dares” presentation, and niche positioning. The overall impression is [highly / substantially] similar.

3. **Proximity of the goods**  
   Both products are [the same or closely related]—party/game products aimed at the same segment (e.g., girls’ sleepover). They compete in the same product category and search environment on Amazon.

4. **Actual marketplace context**  
   On Amazon, consumers often encounter products via search results or category browsing, where thumbnail images and short titles dominate. In that context, the similarity of overall visual presentation (main image, colors, layout, product format) increases the risk that consumers will believe the reported product is the same as or affiliated with HAPPY LOLLI.

5. **Target customer overlap**  
   Both listings are directed at the same or overlapping customers (e.g., purchasers of girls’ party/sleepover games). This overlap increases the likelihood that confused consumers would consider and potentially purchase the reported product in the belief it is HAPPY LOLLI or related.
