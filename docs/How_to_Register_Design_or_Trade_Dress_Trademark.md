# How to Get a Design or Trade Dress Registration (U.S.)

This guide covers applying for a **design mark** or **trade dress** registration at the United States Patent and Trademark Office (USPTO) so you can enforce your product/packaging look on Amazon and elsewhere.

**Relevance for HAPPY LOLLI:** A design or trade dress registration for your product packaging (container + sticks, colors, layout) or a distinctive logo/design used on it will strengthen Brand Registry reports against lookalike listings (e.g. ASIN B0G1G5343H).

---

## 1. What to Apply For

| Type | What it protects | Best for |
|------|-------------------|----------|
| **Design mark** | A specific logo, graphic, or stylized element (e.g. label design, icon, combined logo + wording in a fixed layout). | Distinctive logo or label used on packaging or product. |
| **Trade dress (product packaging)** | The overall appearance of packaging: shape, colors, layout, graphics, and how they combine. | Your tub/container + colored sticks + color palette + “60 Truths & Dares” presentation as a whole. |
| **Trade dress (product design)** | The shape/configuration of the product itself. | Harder to register; usually requires **secondary meaning** (proof consumers associate the design with your brand). |

**Practical choice for HAPPY LOLLI:**  
- **Option A:** Register the **packaging trade dress** (overall look of the container, sticks, colors, and main image/label layout). Packaging can be **inherently distinctive**, so you may not need years of use.  
- **Option B:** Register a **design mark** (e.g. the specific label or main image layout as a “design”).  
- **Option C:** Both — one application for packaging trade dress, one for a design mark, if the budget allows.

---

## 2. Where to File

- **Trademark Center** (new): [uspto.gov/trademarks](https://www.uspto.gov/trademarks) — the USPTO is moving to this as the main way to file.  
- **TEAS (Trademark Electronic Application System):** [teas.uspto.gov](https://teas.uspto.gov) — still available; same legal effect.  
- You need a **USPTO.gov account** and **identity verification** (including two-step authentication) to submit.

---

## 3. What You Need for the Application

### 3.1 Drawing (depiction of the mark)

- **Drawing** = what the trademark *is* (the design or trade dress you claim).  
- For **design** or **trade dress**, you file a **special form** drawing (not standard characters):
  - **Design mark:** Clear image of the logo/label (JPG or PNG). No word-only version unless it’s stylized.
  - **Trade dress:** Drawing or photo that shows the **overall appearance** (e.g. packaging front/side, or product in packaging). The USPTO expects a single, consistent depiction. For packaging, a line drawing or photo of the package is typical; you can add a written description (see below).
- **Color:** If you claim color as part of the mark, the drawing should be in color and you must state that color is claimed. Black-and-white gives broader protection (any color); color claim limits you to that palette but can be stronger for enforcement when competitors copy the same colors.
- **One mark per application.** Different designs or different “views” of trade dress = separate applications if you want multiple registrations.

**Reference:** [USPTO – Drawings and specimens](https://www.uspto.gov/trademarks/basics/drawings-and-specimens), [Mark drawings](https://www.uspto.gov/trademarks/basics/mark-drawings-trademarks).

### 3.2 Description of the mark

- In the application, you **describe** the design or trade dress in words (e.g. “The mark consists of the three-dimensional configuration of product packaging comprising a cylindrical container with a clear lid, containing multiple colored sticks, and a label featuring the wording ‘60 Truths & Dares’ in a specific layout and color scheme.”).
- For **trade dress**, the description should capture the **nonfunctional** visual elements (shape, colors, graphics, layout) that create the overall impression. Avoid describing purely functional features.

### 3.3 Specimen (proof of use)

- **Specimen** = proof of **actual use in commerce** (how consumers see the mark in the marketplace).  
- For **goods**, acceptable specimens include:
  - Photo of the **product packaging** with the design/label visible, or  
  - Photo of the **product** with the design on it, or  
  - **Website or Amazon listing image** showing the product/packaging (with URL and date of access).  
- The specimen must show the **same** design/trade dress as in the drawing. For trade dress, a clear photo of the packaging (or a listing image that shows it) is typically used.

**Reference:** [USPTO – Specimen refusals](https://www.uspto.gov/trademarks/laws/specimen-refusal-and-how-overcome-refusal).

### 3.4 Goods and filing basis

- **Goods:** Identify the product (e.g. “party games; truth or dare games; board games”). Use the USPTO’s **ID Manual** or an approved description.  
- **Filing basis:**  
  - **Use in commerce (1(a)):** You are already selling the product with this packaging/design in the U.S. Submit specimen showing current use.  
  - **Intent to use (1(b)):** You are not yet using it; you will submit specimen later (after approval, before registration).  
- For trade dress, **use in commerce** is usually better if you already sell (e.g. on Amazon); the listing and packaging can serve as specimens.

## 4. Distinctiveness: Why It Matters

The USPTO will only register trade dress that is **distinctive** (identifies source).

- **Product packaging trade dress** can be **inherently distinctive** if the overall look is not generic and is not merely functional. The examiner will consider whether it is common in the field, unique, or a “mere refinement” of common packaging.  
- **Product design** (shape of the product itself) is generally **not** inherently distinctive; you usually must show **secondary meaning** (consumer recognition over time, often with advertising, sales, and time in the market).

**For HAPPY LOLLI:** Emphasize **packaging** (container + label + sticks + colors as presented) rather than only the product shape. In the description, stress the **combination** of nonfunctional elements and, if relevant, that the packaging is used in a specific niche (e.g. girls’ party/sleepover games) where it stands out.

---

## 5. Functionality

Trade dress **cannot be functional**. If a feature is “essential to the use or purpose” of the product or “affects cost or quality,” it is not protectable as trade dress.

- **Nonfunctional:** Specific color palette, decorative layout, distinctive label design, nonessential graphics.  
- **Functional:** The fact that the game has a container and sticks (basic format). You are not claiming the generic “tub with sticks” idea; you are claiming your **specific** look (colors, layout, typography, overall impression).

In the application and description, focus on the **ornamental/source-identifying** aspects, not the utilitarian function of holding sticks.

---

## 6. Step-by-Step (Summary)

1. **Decide** what to register: packaging trade dress and/or a design mark (logo/label).  
2. **Search** the USPTO database (TESS) for similar registered/pending marks.  
3. **Prepare**  
   - Drawing (special form): image of the design or packaging.  
   - Written description of the mark (and that color is claimed, if applicable).  
   - Specimen: photo or listing image showing the design/packaging in use.  
   - Goods: precise identification (ID Manual).  
4. **Create** a USPTO.gov account and complete identity verification.  
5. **File** via [Trademark Center](https://www.uspto.gov/trademarks) or [TEAS](https://teas.uspto.gov). Choose “special form” and upload the drawing; add description, specimen, and goods. Pay the current fee (per class; check [USPTO fee schedule](https://www.uspto.gov/trademarks/fees-payment-information/overview-trademark-fees)).  
6. **Respond** to any office actions (e.g. distinctiveness or functionality) with legal arguments and, for secondary meaning, evidence if needed.  
7. **Registration** — once approved and (for 1(b)) specimen accepted, the registration will issue. Use the registration number in Amazon Brand Registry and when reporting infringement.

---

## 7. Using an Attorney

Trade dress and design applications often get **office actions** on distinctiveness, functionality, or description. An IP attorney can:

- Draft the description and responses.  
- Advise on drawing/specimen format.  
- Argue inherent distinctiveness or submit secondary meaning evidence.

For a single class, attorney fees vary; USPTO fees are separate (see [USPTO fees](https://www.uspto.gov/trademarks/fees-payment-information/overview-trademark-fees)).

---

## 8. After Registration

- Add the **design or trade dress registration** to your Amazon Brand Registry account.  
- When reporting lookalike listings (e.g. B0G1G5343H), **select this registration** (not only the word mark) so the report matches your trade dress claim and improves the chance of action.  
- Keep using the same packaging/design consistently so the registration remains valid.
