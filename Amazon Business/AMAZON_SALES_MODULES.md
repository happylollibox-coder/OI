# Amazon Sales Modules / Traffic Sources
## Complete Guide to Where Your Sales Come From

---

## What Are "Sales Modules"?

**Sales Modules** (also called **Traffic Sources** or **Sales Channels**) are the different places and ways customers discover and purchase your products on Amazon. Understanding these modules helps you track where sales come from and optimize your strategy for 10% ACOS.

**Key Point**: Your **Total Sales** = Sum of sales from all modules

---

## Primary Sales Modules on Amazon

### 1. Search Results Module (Organic & Paid) - **40-60%** of total sales

**What It Is**: Products appearing in Amazon search results when customers search for keywords

**Types**:
- **Organic Search**: Natural search results based on Amazon's ranking algorithm
- **Paid Search**: Sponsored Products, Sponsored Brands in search results

**How It's Tracked**:
- **Organic**: Business Reports → Detail Page Sales & Traffic → "Organic search"
- **Paid**: Advertising Console → Campaign Performance Reports
- **Combined**: Brand Analytics → Search Catalog Performance (Brand Registered only)

**Why It Matters**:
- Largest traffic source for most sellers (40-60% of total)
- Search results = High purchase intent
- Organic search = Lower ACOS (no ad costs)
- Paid search = Must optimize for ACOS

**Optimization for 10% ACOS**:
- Improve organic ranking (SEO, keywords, reviews)
- Optimize paid search campaigns (negative keywords, exact match)
- Track Search Catalog Performance metrics

---

### 2. Detail Page / Product Page Module - **2-5%** of total sales

**What It Is**: Sales from customers landing directly on your product detail page

**Includes**:
- Direct product links (from external sites, social media, email)
- Bookmarks/favorites
- Direct navigation to your product URL

**How It's Tracked**:
- Business Reports → Detail Page Sales & Traffic → "Direct" or "External" traffic
- Can track via UTM parameters for external links

**Why It Matters**:
- High conversion rate (customers already interested)
- Often from external marketing (social media, email, blog)
- Lower ad dependency = Lower TACOS

**Optimization for 10% ACOS**:
- Drive external traffic to your listings (social media, email marketing)
- Use UTM tracking to measure external campaigns
- Optimize detail pages for conversion (A+ Content, reviews)

---

### 3. Browse / Category Module - **15-30%** of total sales

**What It Is**: Sales from customers browsing Amazon categories, browse nodes, or navigation menus

**Includes**:
- Category browsing (e.g., Home & Kitchen → Kitchen & Dining → Cookware)
- Best Seller pages
- New Releases pages
- Category bestseller rankings

**How It's Tracked**:
- Business Reports → Detail Page Sales & Traffic → "Browse" traffic
- Business Reports → Detail Page Sales & Traffic → Category views

**Why It Matters**:
- Second-largest traffic source (15-30% of total)
- Lower competition than search results
- Category ranking helps visibility

**Optimization for 10% ACOS**:
- Ensure correct category placement
- Aim for category bestseller badges
- Optimize for category browse paths

---

### 4. Sponsored Products / Display Ads Module - **15-25%** of total sales

**What It Is**: Sales attributed to Amazon advertising placements

**Includes**:
- **Sponsored Products**: Ads in search results and on product pages
- **Sponsored Brands**: Brand ads with logo and multiple products
- **Sponsored Display**: Retargeting and product targeting ads

**How It's Tracked**:
- Advertising Console → Campaign Performance
- Business Reports → Detail Page Sales & Traffic (ad-attributed sessions)

**Why It Matters**:
- Directly affects ACOS (ad spend vs ad sales)
- Can be measured precisely
- Critical for achieving 10% ACOS goal

**Optimization for 10% ACOS**:
- Monitor ACOS by campaign, keyword, ASIN
- Use negative keywords aggressively
- Optimize bids and budgets
- Focus spend on high-converting keywords/products

---

### 5. "Other ASIN" / Related Products Module - **5-10%** of total sales

**What It Is**: Sales from customers clicking on your product from other product pages

**Includes**:
- "Frequently bought together" sections
- "Customers who viewed this also viewed" widgets
- "Sponsored Products related to this item" (on competitor pages)
- Cross-sell/upsell recommendations

**How It's Tracked**:
- Business Reports → Detail Page Sales & Traffic → "Other ASIN" traffic
- Advertising Reports → Placement Report (for sponsored placements)

**Why It Matters**:
- Incremental sales from product page recommendations
- Lower cost than search ads (if organic recommendations)
- Can use Sponsored Display for competitor conquesting

**Optimization for 10% ACOS**:
- Optimize for "Frequently bought together" (bundles, complementary products)
- Use Sponsored Display for ASIN targeting (if profitable)
- Improve product relationships (category, keywords)

---

### 6. Brand Store / Storefront Module - **2-5%** of total sales

**What It Is**: Sales from customers visiting your Amazon Brand Store

**Includes**:
- Brand Store visits
- Products purchased from Brand Store pages
- Multi-product browsing within your brand

**How It's Tracked**:
- Brand Analytics → Brand Store Performance (Brand Registered only)
- Business Reports → Store Performance (if available)

**Why It Matters**:
- Builds brand awareness and loyalty
- Higher average order value (customers browse multiple products)
- Lower ACOS (organic traffic to store)

**Optimization for 10% ACOS**:
- Create/optimize Brand Store (if Brand Registered)
- Drive traffic to store from ads (Sponsored Brands with store landing pages)
- Cross-promote products within store

**Requirement**: Amazon Brand Registry enrollment

---

### 7. Deals & Promotions Module - **5-15%** of total sales

**What It Is**: Sales from Amazon deals, lightning deals, coupons, and promotions

**Includes**:
- Lightning Deals
- Deal of the Day
- Prime Day deals
- Coupons (Amazon Coupons)
- Promotions (BOGO, percentage off, etc.)

**How It's Tracked**:
- Business Reports → Sales and Traffic → Filter by promotion
- Advertising Console → Promotions dashboard (if using sponsored promotions)

**Why It Matters**:
- Can drive significant volume spikes
- Lower margins (discounts) but higher sales
- Can help with ranking and reviews

**Optimization for 10% ACOS**:
- Calculate profitability (discount + ad costs)
- Use deals strategically (new launches, clearance)
- Combine with ads for maximum visibility
- Monitor TACOS during promotions (not just ACOS)

---

### 8. Amazon's Choice / Featured Offer Module - **80-90%** of sales go through Buy Box

**What It Is**: Sales from winning "Amazon's Choice" badge or Featured Offer (Buy Box)

**Includes**:
- Amazon's Choice badge in search results
- Featured Offer / Buy Box placement
- Prime-eligible badges

**How It's Tracked**:
- Business Reports → Detail Page Sales & Traffic → Buy Box % column
- Inventory Performance Dashboard → Buy Box percentage
- Search Catalog Performance (if Brand Registered)

**Why It Matters**:
- **Critical**: 80-90% of sales go through Buy Box
- Losing Buy Box = 70-80% sales loss
- Amazon's Choice = Increased visibility in search

**Optimization for 10% ACOS**:
- Maintain Buy Box eligibility (>95% win rate)
- Ensure FBA/Prime fulfillment (helps Buy Box)
- Maintain excellent seller metrics (>90% rating)
- Competitive pricing (within 5-10% of competitors)

### 9. Video / A+ Content Module - **Influences 5-10%** conversion rate lift

**What It Is**: Sales influenced by video content or A+ Content modules on product pages

**Includes**:
- Product videos (main image or A+ Content)
- A+ Content modules (Enhanced Brand Content)
- A+ Premium Content (Shoppable images, comparison charts)

**How It's Tracked**:
- Indirectly via conversion rate improvements
- Business Reports → Detail Page Sales & Traffic (conversion rate lift)
- Brand Analytics → A+ Content metrics (if available)

**Why It Matters**:
- Improves conversion rate 5-10%
- Better conversion = Lower ACOS
- Professional content builds trust

**Optimization for 10% ACOS**:
- Add product videos to main images
- Create comprehensive A+ Content
- Use A+ Premium for shoppable elements
- Test different content modules

**Requirement**: Brand Registry for A+ Content

### 10. External Traffic Module - **3-8%** of total sales

**What It Is**: Sales from traffic coming from outside Amazon

**Includes**:
- Social media (Facebook, Instagram, TikTok)
- Email marketing campaigns
- Blog posts or articles
- Google search (external SEO)
- Direct links from your website

**How It's Tracked**:
- Business Reports → Detail Page Sales & Traffic → "External" or "Other" sources
- UTM parameters for campaign tracking
- Amazon Attribution (for tracking external campaigns)

**Why It Matters**:
- Lower ad costs (external marketing)
- Builds brand awareness
- Diversifies traffic sources
- Reduces dependency on Amazon ads

**Optimization for 10% ACOS**:
- Use Amazon Attribution to track external campaigns
- Drive external traffic via social media, email
- Create content marketing (blog, videos)
- Use UTM tracking for all external links

### 11. Recommendations / Personalization Module - **2-5%** of total sales

**What It Is**: Sales from Amazon's recommendation algorithms

**Includes**:
- "Recommended for you" sections
- Personalized home page recommendations
- Recently viewed items
- "You might also like" widgets
- Product recommendations based on browsing history

**How It's Tracked**:
- Business Reports → Detail Page Sales & Traffic → "Recommendations" or "Personalized"
- Indirectly via conversion rate on product pages

**Why It Matters**:
- Passive organic traffic (no ad spend)
- Highly targeted (based on customer behavior)
- Incremental sales

**Optimization for 10% ACOS**:
- Optimize listings for relevance (keywords, category)
- Build sales velocity (more sales = more recommendations)
- Ensure high conversion rates (Amazon favors converting products)

---

### 12. Subscribe & Save / Recurring Orders Module - **1-5%** of total sales (varies by product type)

**What It Is**: Sales from Amazon's Subscribe & Save program

**Includes**:
- Recurring subscriptions
- Subscribe & Save discounts
- Scheduled deliveries

**How It's Tracked**:
- Business Reports → Subscribe & Save performance
- Order reports → Filter by subscription orders

**Why It Matters**:
- Recurring revenue (predictable)
- Lower ACOS (repeat customers, no new ad spend)
- Customer loyalty

**Optimization for 10% ACOS**:
- Enroll eligible products in Subscribe & Save
- Offer competitive subscription discounts
- Ensure product quality (reduces cancellations)

## Tracking Sales by Module

### Where to Find Module Data:

#### 1. Business Reports → Sales and Traffic

**Location**: Reports → Business Reports → Sales and Traffic by Parent ASIN

**Shows**:
- Sessions by traffic source (Search, Browse, Other ASIN, etc.)
- Page views by source
- Units ordered by source (estimated)

#### 2. Brand Analytics (Brand Registered Only)

**Available Reports**:
- **Search Catalog Performance**: Search impressions, clicks, conversion
- **Brand Store Performance**: Store visits and sales
- **Search Query Performance**: Keyword-level performance
- **Market Basket Analysis**: What customers buy together
- **Item Comparison**: How your products compare to competitors

#### 3. Advertising Console

**Available Reports**:
- **Campaign Performance**: Ad-attributed sales by campaign type
- **Placement Report**: Performance by placement (search, product pages)
- **Search Term Report**: Keyword-level ad performance

#### 4. Inventory Performance Dashboard

**Shows**:
- Buy Box percentage (indirect module indicator)
- Conversion rates (influenced by module performance)

## How Sales Modules Relate to ACOS

### Understanding ACOS vs TACOS by Module:

**ACOS (Advertising Cost of Sale)**:
- Only includes **ad-attributed sales** (Paid Search, Display Ads modules)
- Formula: `ACOS = (Ad Spend ÷ Ad Sales) × 100%`

**TACOS (Total Advertising Cost of Sale)**:
- Includes **all sales** (all modules)
- Formula: `TACOS = (Ad Spend ÷ Total Sales) × 100%`

**Relationship**:
```
Total Sales = Organic Search + Paid Ads + Browse + Other ASIN + 
              Brand Store + External + Deals + Other Modules

If:
- Ad Sales = $10,000
- Ad Spend = $1,000
- Total Sales = $50,000

Then:
- ACOS = ($1,000 ÷ $10,000) × 100% = 10% ✅
- TACOS = ($1,000 ÷ $50,000) × 100% = 2% ✅

Lower TACOS = Better (because organic and other modules help)
```

### Why This Matters for 10% ACOS Goal:

**Scenario A: Low Organic, High Ad Dependency**
- Total Sales: $20,000
- Ad Sales: $18,000 (90% of total)
- Ad Spend: $1,800
- **ACOS = 10%** ✅
- **TACOS = 9%** ⚠️ (high dependency on ads)

**Scenario B: High Organic, Low Ad Dependency**
- Total Sales: $50,000
- Ad Sales: $10,000 (20% of total)
- Ad Spend: $1,000
- **ACOS = 10%** ✅
- **TACOS = 2%** ✅ (healthy organic mix)

**Goal**: Build organic modules (Search, Browse, Recommendations) to reduce ad dependency and improve TACOS while maintaining ACOS at 10%

## Optimizing Modules for 10% ACOS

### Strategy by Module:

#### Modules to Maximize (Low/No Ad Costs):

1. **Organic Search**
   - Optimize SEO (title, bullets, backend keywords)
   - Build sales velocity (ads help initially)
   - Improve reviews and ratings

2. **Browse/Category**
   - Ensure correct category placement
   - Aim for category bestseller badges
   - Optimize for browse paths

3. **Other ASIN / Recommendations**
   - Create bundles/complementary products
   - Optimize product relationships
   - Build sales velocity

4. **External Traffic**
   - Drive traffic via social media, email
   - Content marketing (blog, videos)
   - Build brand awareness

#### Modules to Optimize (Ad Costs):

1. **Paid Search**
   - Focus: Achieve 10% ACOS
   - Use negative keywords aggressively
   - Optimize bids and budgets
   - Move winners to exact match

2. **Sponsored Display**
   - Monitor closely (can be expensive)
   - Use for retargeting (lower cost)
   - ASIN targeting only if profitable

3. **Deals/Promotions**
   - Calculate total cost (discount + ads)
   - Use strategically (not constant)
   - Monitor TACOS during promotions

**Key Questions**:
1. Which modules are driving most sales?
2. Which modules have best conversion rates?
3. Are organic modules growing?
4. Are ad modules achieving 10% ACOS?
5. Which modules need optimization?

## Summary: Key Takeaways

### Understanding Sales Modules Helps You:

1. ✅ **Track Total Sales Sources**: Know where every sale comes from
2. ✅ **Optimize ACOS**: Focus ad spend on efficient modules
3. ✅ **Improve TACOS**: Build organic modules to reduce ad dependency
4. ✅ **Identify Opportunities**: Find underperforming modules to improve
5. ✅ **Allocate Budget**: Shift spend between modules based on performance

### For 10% ACOS Goal:

- **Maximize Organic Modules**: Search, Browse, Recommendations (no ad costs)
- **Optimize Paid Modules**: Search Ads, Display Ads (achieve 10% ACOS)
- **Monitor TACOS**: Lower TACOS = Better organic mix = More sustainable
- **Balance Strategy**: Don't rely too heavily on any single module

**Bottom Line**: Your Total Sales come from multiple modules. Understanding each module helps you optimize for both ACOS (ad efficiency) and TACOS (overall efficiency).
