## Core Metrics & Definitions

### ACOS (Advertising Cost of Sales)
**Formula**: `ACOS = (Ad Spend ÷ Ad Revenue) × 100%`

- **What it means**: Percentage of ad-attributed sales revenue spent on advertising
- **10% ACOS means**: Spending $0.10 in ads for every $1.00 in ad-driven sales
- **ROAS relationship**: 10% ACOS = 10x ROAS ($10 revenue per $1 spent)

### TACOS (Total Advertising Cost of Sales)
**Formula**: `TACOS = (Ad Spend ÷ Total Sales) × 100%`

- **Total Sales** includes both organic + ad-driven sales
- **Why it matters**: Even if ACOS is 10%, if organic sales are low, TACOS could be higher
- **Goal**: Monitor both metrics - organic growth reduces TACOS naturally

### Break-Even ACOS
**Formula**: `Break-Even ACOS = Profit Margin (after all non-ad costs)`

- **Calculation needed**:
  - Product Cost (COGS)
  - Amazon Referral Fees
  - FBA Fees (pick, pack, ship, storage)
  - Shipping to FBA
  - Packaging
  - Returns/Refunds allowance
  - Promotions/Discounts
  - Other overhead

**Example**:
- Selling Price: $50
- Total Costs (non-ad): $30
- Profit Margin: ($50 - $30) / $50 = **40%**
- Break-Even ACOS: **40%** (can spend up to 40% on ads and still profit)
- Target 10% ACOS: Leaves **30% net profit** after ads

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

| Metric | Impact on ACOS | Target for 10% ACOS |
|--------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Conversion Rate (CVR)** | Higher = Lower ACOS | 10-15%+ (varies by category) |
| **Cost Per Click (CPC)** | Lower = Lower ACOS | As low as possible while maintaining position |
| **Click-Through Rate (CTR)** | Higher = Better relevance, lower CPC | 0.3-1.0%+ (varies) |
| **Average Order Value (AOV)** | Higher = Better margin absorption | Maximize via bundles/upsells |

## The Math Behind 10% ACOS

### Required Conditions

To consistently achieve 10% ACOS, you typically need:

1. **High Profit Margins**: 30%+ margin after all costs (before ads)
2. **Strong Conversion Rates**: 10-15%+ conversion on landing pages
3. **Relevant Keyword Targeting**: Long-tail, high-intent keywords
4. **Optimized Listings**: High-quality images, A+ content, reviews
5. **Competitive Pricing**: Not the lowest, but competitive with value
6. **Buy Box Eligibility**: Consistent Buy Box ownership

### Why 10% is Challenging

- **Industry Average**: Most sellers see 15-30% ACOS as "good"
- **Competition**: High-competition categories have expensive CPCs
- **Seasonality**: Q4 typically sees higher CPCs and ACOS
- **Product Lifecycle**: New products need higher ACOS initially

### When 10% ACOS is Realistic

✅ **High-margin products** (luxury, branded, specialized)
✅ **Established listings** with reviews and organic ranking
✅ **Branded keyword campaigns** (your own brand terms)
✅ **Niche markets** with lower competition
✅ **Products with high AOV** ($50+ average order)

❌ **Commodity products** with thin margins
❌ **New launches** without reviews/ranking
❌ **Highly competitive categories** (electronics, toys)
❌ **Low-priced items** (<$10)

## Fundamental Pillars for Success

### 1. Product Economics & Margins

**Critical Calculations**:
```
Total Unit Cost = 
  COGS + 
  FBA Fees + 
  Amazon Referral Fee + 
  Shipping to FBA + 
  Packaging + 
  Returns Allowance + 
  Promotions + 
  Storage Fees

Break-Even ACOS = ((Selling Price - Total Unit Cost) / Selling Price) × 100
Target ACOS = Break-Even ACOS - Desired Profit Margin
```

**Action Items**:
- Calculate break-even ACOS for every product
- Only advertise products where break-even ACOS > 15%
- Monitor margin changes (fee increases, cost inflation)
- Review monthly margin reports

### 2. Inventory Management & FBA

**Amazon's Algorithm Signals**:
- **Stock Levels**: Out-of-stock = ranking penalty = lower conversion = higher ACOS
- **Buy Box Eligibility**: Lose Buy Box = fewer sales per click = higher ACOS
- **Inventory Health**: Stranded inventory = wasted ad spend
- **IPI Score**: Affects storage limits and fees

**Best Practices**:
- Maintain 30-60 days of inventory at FBA
- Monitor reorder points and lead times
- Avoid stockouts during ad campaigns
- Use FBA Multi-Channel Fulfillment efficiently
- Monitor long-term storage fees (remove slow movers)

### 3. Listing Quality & Conversion

**Conversion Rate Impact**:
- Higher CVR = Lower ACOS (more sales per click)
- Listing optimization is the #1 lever for ACOS improvement

**Critical Listing Elements**:

1. **Title**: Primary keywords + benefits, 200 characters max
2. **Images**: 7+ high-quality images, lifestyle + infographic
3. **Bullet Points**: Benefits-focused, keyword-rich, scannable
4. **Description**: Detailed, keyword-optimized (SEO)
5. **A+ Content**: Increased conversion 5-10% (Brand Registry required)
6. **Backend Keywords**: Hidden SEO keywords (250 bytes)
7. **Reviews**: 4.5+ stars, 100+ reviews optimal
8. **Q&A Section**: Address common concerns proactively

**Conversion Optimization Checklist**:
- [ ] Professional product photography (lifestyle + detail shots)
- [ ] Clear value propositions in bullets
- [ ] Social proof (reviews, ratings, badges)
- [ ] Competitive pricing (within 5-10% of top competitors)
- [ ] Fast shipping (Prime eligible)
- [ ] Enhanced Brand Content / A+ Content
- [ ] Video (in main image or A+ content)

### 4. Campaign Architecture

**Three-Layer Campaign Structure**:

**Layer 1: Discovery**
- Purpose: Find new keywords and audiences
- Match Types: Broad, Auto
- ACOS Expectation: 25-40% (acceptable for learning)
- Budget: 10-20% of total ad spend

**Layer 2: Optimization**
- Purpose: Scale proven keywords
- Match Types: Phrase, Exact
- ACOS Expectation: 15-25% (improving)
- Budget: 30-40% of total ad spend

**Layer 3: Profit**
- Purpose: Maximum efficiency keywords
- Match Types: Exact, Branded
- ACOS Expectation: <10% (target)
- Budget: 40-50% of total ad spend

**Campaign Segmentation**:
- Separate by product tier (A/B/C products)
- Separate by match type (Exact/Phrase/Broad)
- Separate by purpose (Brand/Competitor/Generic)
- Separate by ASIN (high performers get dedicated campaigns)

## Amazon Seller Algorithm Logic

### How Amazon Ranks Products

**Primary Ranking Factors**:
1. **Sales Velocity**: Units sold per time period
2. **Relevance**: Keyword match + conversion rate
3. **Performance Metrics**: Order Defect Rate, Cancellation Rate, Late Shipment
4. **Customer Satisfaction**: Reviews, ratings, return rate
5. **Inventory Availability**: Stock levels, FBA status
6. **Pricing Competitiveness**: Within competitive range
7. **Advertising Performance**: ACOS, conversion from ads

### Buy Box Eligibility

**Critical for ACOS**: Losing Buy Box = higher ACOS

**Buy Box Factors**:
- Professional seller account
- High seller rating (>90%)
- Order defect rate <1%
- Late shipment rate <4%
- Cancellation rate <2.5%
- Sufficient inventory
- Competitive pricing
- Shipping performance (FBA helps)

### How Ads Affect Organic Ranking

**Direct Impact**:
- Ads generate sales → improve sales velocity → boost organic ranking
- Higher organic ranking → lower need for ads → lower ACOS over time
- **Strategy**: Use ads to bootstrap organic, then reduce ad spend as organic grows

**Indirect Impact**:
- Ads increase impressions → brand awareness
- Ads drive reviews → social proof → better conversion
- Ads test keywords → validate demand → optimize listings

## Campaign Structure & Strategy

### Campaign Types & Use Cases

#### 1. Sponsored Products (Most Common)
**Best For**: Individual product promotion
**Control**: Keyword, ASIN, category targeting
**ACOS Potential**: 10-30% typical, <10% achievable

#### 2. Sponsored Brands
**Best For**: Brand awareness, multi-product promotion
**Control**: Branded keywords, landing pages
**ACOS Potential**: 15-35% typical

#### 3. Sponsored Display
**Best For**: Retargeting, competitor conquesting
**Control**: Audience, product, view remarketing
**ACOS Potential**: 20-40% typical

### Recommended Campaign Structure

**Week 1-2: Baseline & Cleanup**
```
1. Audit existing campaigns
   - Identify ACOS by campaign/product/keyword
   - Find high-spend, low-return keywords
   - Calculate break-even ACOS per product

2. Negative Keyword Mining
   - Pull Search Term Report (30-90 days)
   - Identify non-converting terms with spend
   - Add as campaign/ad group negatives

3. Campaign Segmentation
   - Separate by product tier (High/Med/Low performers)
   - Separate by match type (Exact/Phrase/Broad)
   - Separate by purpose (Brand/Generic/Competitor)
```

**Week 3-4: Optimization & Restructuring**
```
1. Move Winners to Exact Match
   - Extract keywords with ACOS < 15%
   - Create new Exact Match campaigns
   - Increase bids 10-20% for winners

2. Bid Adjustments
   - Reduce bids on ACOS > target × 1.3
   - Increase bids on ACOS < target
   - Use "Dynamic - Down Only" for tighter control

3. Budget Reallocation
   - Shift budget from high-ACOS to low-ACOS campaigns
   - Prioritize high-margin products
   - Cap budgets on test campaigns
```

**Week 5-8: Scale & Refinement**
```
1. Scale Winners Carefully
   - Increase budgets 20-30% per week on low-ACOS campaigns
   - Monitor ACOS drift (should stay < 15%)
   - Pause if ACOS exceeds target significantly

2. Continuous Optimization
   - Weekly negative keyword additions
   - Monthly bid adjustments
   - Quarterly campaign restructures
```

## Keyword Management

### Match Type Strategy

#### Broad Match
- **Use**: Discovery, new product launches
- **ACOS Expectation**: 20-40%
- **Control**: Limited (Amazon expands)
- **When to Use**: Week 1-4 of campaigns, finding new terms

#### Phrase Match
- **Use**: Scaling proven broad terms
- **ACOS Expectation**: 15-25%
- **Control**: Moderate (word order matters)
- **When to Use**: Terms converting from broad, expanding reach

#### Exact Match
- **Use**: Maximum efficiency, proven winners
- **ACOS Expectation**: <15% (target <10%)
- **Control**: Maximum (precise matching)
- **When to Use**: Keywords consistently performing well

#### Auto Campaigns
- **Use**: Discovery, finding new keywords
- **ACOS Expectation**: 20-40%
- **Control**: Minimal (Amazon decides)
- **When to Use**: Always running in background for discovery

### Negative Keyword Strategy

**Why Negatives Are Critical**:
- Block irrelevant searches that waste budget
- Improve ACOS by eliminating non-converting clicks
- Should be 30-50% of total keywords (or more)

**When to Add Negatives**:
- Weekly: Review Search Term Report for non-converters
- Immediate: Terms with clicks but zero sales
- Proactive: Category mismatches (e.g., "free", "cheap", "used")

**Negative Keyword Types**:
1. **Campaign-Level**: Apply to all ad groups (broad exclusions)
2. **Ad Group-Level**: Specific to product type
3. **Exact Negatives**: Specific unwanted terms

**Example Negative Lists**:
- Price qualifiers: "cheap", "free", "discount", "sale"
- Competitor brands: "brand x", "competitor y"
- Wrong product type: "used", "refurbished", "vintage"
- Wrong use case: "diy", "repair kit" (if selling complete product)

### Search Term Report Analysis

**Weekly Process**:
1. Download Search Term Report (30-90 day lookback)
2. Sort by Spend (highest first)
3. Identify terms with:
   - High spend ($10+) and zero sales
   - High spend and ACOS > target × 2
   - Low conversion rate (<1%)
4. Add top offenders as negatives
5. Extract converting terms to add as Exact Match keywords

**Key Metrics to Track**:
- Impressions (reach)
- Clicks (traffic)
- Spend (cost)
- Orders (conversions)
- ACOS (efficiency)
- Conversion Rate (relevance)

## Bidding Strategy & Optimization

### Bid Types

#### Fixed Bids
- **Best For**: Tighter ACOS control, proven keywords
- **Control**: Maximum (you set exact bid)
- **Use When**: ACOS target <15%, data available

#### Dynamic Bids - Down Only
- **Best For**: Maintaining efficiency while allowing optimization
- **Control**: Amazon can only lower bids
- **Use When**: Trying to reduce ACOS, maintaining position

#### Dynamic Bids - Up and Down
- **Best For**: Growth phase, new campaigns
- **Control**: Amazon adjusts both ways
- **Use When**: Accepting higher ACOS for volume, discovery phase

### Bid Placement Adjustments

**Placement Types**:
1. **Top of Search**: Highest visibility, highest cost (usually)
2. **Rest of Search**: Mid-page results
3. **Product Pages**: Competitor/related product pages

**Strategy for 10% ACOS**:
- **Top of Search**: +0% to -20% (often overpriced)
- **Rest of Search**: +0% to +10% (good value)
- **Product Pages**: -20% to +0% (depends on product complementarity)

**Testing Approach**:
- Start with all placements at 0%
- Monitor ACOS by placement (weekly)
- Adjust down if placement ACOS > target
- Adjust up if placement ACOS < target and has volume

### Bid Optimization Process

**Weekly Bid Review**:
```
For each keyword:
  IF ACOS < Target ACOS × 0.7:
    → Increase bid 10-20% (capture more volume)
  
  IF ACOS > Target ACOS × 1.3:
    → Decrease bid 10-20% (reduce spend)
  
  IF ACOS > Target ACOS × 2.0:
    → Pause keyword (unless strategic)
  
  IF Spend < $5/week but ACOS < Target:
    → Increase bid significantly (scale winner)
```

**Monthly Bid Review**:
- Re-evaluate all paused keywords
- Check for new search terms to target
- Review placement performance
- Adjust campaign budgets based on performance

### Dayparting (Time-of-Day Bidding)

**Concept**: Run ads during high-conversion hours

**Implementation**:
- Analyze conversion by hour (via reports)
- Identify peak conversion windows
- Adjust bids +20-50% during peak hours
- Reduce bids -50% during low-conversion hours
- Or pause entirely during dead hours

**Typical Patterns**:
- Weekdays: 9 AM - 5 PM (business hours)
- Evenings: 7 PM - 10 PM (after work)
- Weekends: Saturday mornings, Sunday afternoons

## Listing & Conversion Optimization

### Product Page Optimization Checklist

#### Images (7+ Images Required)
- [ ] Main image: White background, product only, 1000x1000px minimum
- [ ] Image 2-3: Lifestyle/use case images
- [ ] Image 4-5: Features/benefits (infographic style)
- [ ] Image 6-7: Dimensions, comparison, additional angles
- [ ] Video: Optional but recommended (in main image slot or A+)

#### Title Optimization
**Formula**: `[Brand] + [Product Type] + [Key Feature 1] + [Key Feature 2] + [Size/Quantity]`

**Best Practices**:
- Front-load primary keywords (first 50 characters most important)
- Include brand name (for Brand Registry benefits)
- Use natural language (readable, not keyword-stuffed)
- Max 200 characters
- Include size/quantity if relevant

**Example Good Title**:
```
"EcoWater Premium 64oz Water Bottle - Insulated Stainless Steel 
Leak-Proof with Straw - BPA Free - Keeps Cold 24hrs Hot 12hrs"
```

#### Bullet Points (5 Bullets Max)
**Format**: Benefit-focused, not feature-focused

**Formula per bullet**: `[Key Benefit] + [Supporting Detail] + [Emotional Appeal]`

**Example Good Bullet**:
```
"✅ STAYS COLD FOR 24 HOURS - Triple-wall insulation keeps drinks 
ice-cold all day, perfect for hot summer days and intense workouts"
```

**Keywords**: Include primary keywords naturally in 2-3 bullets

#### Description (Backend SEO)
- First 250 characters appear in search results
- Use full character limit (2000+ characters)
- Include keyword variations naturally
- Use HTML formatting (bold, line breaks)
- Include additional features/benefits not in bullets

#### A+ Content (Enhanced Brand Content)
**Impact**: +5-10% conversion rate increase

**Best Practices**:
- Brand story and mission
- Comparison charts (vs competitors)
- Detailed feature explanations
- Lifestyle imagery
- Customer testimonials/use cases
- Technical specifications
- FAQs answered visually

**Requirement**: Brand Registry enrollment

#### Backend Keywords
- 250 bytes limit (not characters)
- Include synonyms, misspellings, alternative names
- Don't repeat keywords in title/bullets
- Use commas, spaces (both work)
- No brand names (unless yours)
- No offensive terms

**Example**:
```
"water bottle, hydration bottle, sports bottle, insulated bottle, 
stainless steel bottle, travel bottle, gym bottle, reusable bottle"
```

### Review & Rating Strategy

**Impact on ACOS**:
- 4.5+ stars = Higher conversion = Lower ACOS
- 100+ reviews = Social proof = Lower ACOS
- Review velocity = Ranking signal = Organic growth

**Strategies to Build Reviews**:
1. **Amazon Vine**: Get early reviews (Brand Registry required)
2. **Request a Review Button**: Automated follow-up emails
3. **Product Inserts**: Polite review requests (within Amazon TOS)
4. **Excellent Customer Service**: Reduces negative reviews
5. **Quality Products**: Fewer returns = better reviews

**Managing Negative Reviews**:
- Respond professionally to all reviews
- Address concerns in product updates
- Use negative feedback to improve listings
- Report policy violations to Amazon

## FBA Inventory & Operations

### FBA Fee Structure Impact

**Understanding FBA Costs**:
1. **Fulfillment Fees**: Per-unit (size/weight based)
2. **Storage Fees**: Monthly (standard vs oversized)
3. **Long-Term Storage Fees**: Items stored 365+ days
4. **Removal/Disposal Fees**: If you need to remove inventory
5. **Returns Processing**: Free for customer-fault, fee for seller-fault

**Impact on ACOS**:
- Higher FBA fees = Lower margins = Need lower ACOS to profit
- Calculate total cost per unit including ALL FBA fees
- Monitor fee changes (Amazon updates annually)

### Inventory Health Metrics

**IPI (Inventory Performance Index)**:
- Score: 0-1000 (400+ required to avoid limits)
- Factors: Excess inventory, sell-through, in-stock rate, stranded inventory
- **Impact**: Low IPI = storage limits = stockout risk = higher ACOS

**Maintaining Healthy IPI**:
- Keep sell-through rate >8 (8+ units sold per month per unit in stock)
- Reduce excess inventory (90+ days without sales)
- Fix stranded inventory (no active listings)
- Maintain in-stock rate >95%

### Buy Box Optimization

**Why Buy Box Matters for ACOS**:
- Lose Buy Box = Only ~30% of sales = Lower conversion = Higher ACOS
- FBA sellers typically win Buy Box >95% of time
- Maintaining Buy Box = Maximum conversion = Lower ACOS

**Buy Box Factors**:
1. **Fulfillment Method**: FBA > FBM (generally)
2. **Shipping Speed**: Prime > Standard
3. **Price**: Competitive (not necessarily lowest)
4. **Seller Performance**: >90% positive feedback
5. **Order Defect Rate**: <1%
6. **Inventory Availability**: In stock

## Advanced Strategies for 10% ACOS

### 1. Portfolio Approach
- **High-Margin Products**: Target 8-10% ACOS
- **Medium-Margin Products**: Target 15-20% ACOS
- **Low-Margin Products**: Limit ads or accept 25-30% ACOS
- **Overall Portfolio ACOS**: Weighted average targeting 10%

### 2. Brand Defense Campaigns
- Create campaigns for your brand name + variations
- These typically achieve 5-8% ACOS (very efficient)
- Prevents competitors from stealing branded traffic
- Should be 10-20% of total ad budget

### 3. Competitor Conquesting (Strategic)
- Target competitor ASINs where you have advantage
- Use only for high-margin products
- Monitor closely (can be expensive)
- ACOS target: 12-15% (slightly higher than branded)

### 4. Seasonal Adjustments
- **Q4 (Holiday)**: Expect 15-25% ACOS (higher competition)
- **Q1-Q3**: Target 10% ACOS
- **Back-to-School**: Category-specific spikes
- Plan budget increases for high-volume periods

### 5. New Product Launch Strategy
- **Month 1**: Accept 30-40% ACOS (learning phase)
- **Month 2**: Target 20-25% ACOS (optimization)
- **Month 3**: Target 15% ACOS (refinement)
- **Month 4+**: Target 10% ACOS (maturity)
